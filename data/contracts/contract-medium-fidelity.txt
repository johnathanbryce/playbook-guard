MASTER SUBSCRIPTION AGREEMENT — Meridian Cloud Services, Inc. ("Provider") and Customer

1. DEFINITIONS
1.1 "Service" means Provider's hosted Meridian workflow platform, including its AI-assisted features. "Customer Content" means data, inputs, and materials submitted to the Service by or on behalf of Customer. "DPA" means the Data Processing Addendum referenced in Section 6.

2. SUBSCRIPTION AND LICENSE
2.1 Provider grants Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable right to access and use the Service during the Term for its internal business purposes, subject to this Agreement and the applicable Order Form.

3. CUSTOMER RESPONSIBILITIES
3.1 Customer is responsible for the legality of Customer Content, for use of the Service by its authorized users, and for safeguarding its account credentials.

4. FEES AND PAYMENT
4.1 Customer shall pay the annual subscription fee of $140,000 as set forth in the Order Form, invoiced annually in advance.
4.2 Invoices are due within forty-five (45) days of the invoice date (Net 45). Undisputed late amounts accrue interest at 1.0% per month.
4.3 Fees for the then-current term are fixed and may not be changed during that term. All fees are exclusive of taxes.

5. TERM, AUTO-RENEWAL AND PRICING
5.1 The subscription term is twelve (12) months from the subscription start date stated in the Order Form.
5.2 The subscription automatically renews for successive twelve (12) month terms unless either party gives written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days before the end of the then-current term.
5.3 Provider will send Customer written notice of renewal pricing at least sixty (60) days before each renewal date.
5.4 Provider may increase fees at each renewal by no more than seven percent (7%) over the fees for the immediately preceding term.

6. DATA PROCESSING AND PRIVACY
6.1 The parties' Data Processing Addendum, which satisfies the requirements of GDPR Article 28, is incorporated into this Agreement and governs Provider's processing of personal data within Customer Content.
6.2 Where Provider transfers personal data out of the European Economic Area, Switzerland, or the United Kingdom, the transfer is governed by the Standard Contractual Clauses incorporated into the DPA.
6.3 Provider maintains a list of its sub-processors and will give Customer written notice at least twenty (20) days before adding or replacing a sub-processor. Customer may object in writing on reasonable data-protection grounds within that notice period, and the parties will work in good faith to resolve the objection.
6.4 Provider will notify Customer of any personal data breach affecting Customer Content without undue delay and in any event within seventy-two (72) hours of becoming aware of the breach.

7. AI FEATURES; TRAINING AND OUTPUT RIGHTS
7.1 The Service includes AI-assisted features ("AI Features"). Provider will not use Customer Content, inputs to the AI Features, or outputs generated by the AI Features to train, fine-tune, or improve any machine-learning model.
7.2 As between the parties, Customer owns all Customer Content, all inputs it submits to the AI Features, and all outputs generated by the AI Features for Customer.
7.3 Provider may generate and use aggregated, de-identified usage metadata (such as feature-usage counts and performance statistics) to operate and improve the Service, provided such metadata does not contain Customer Content and Provider does not attempt to re-identify any individual or Customer from it.

8. SERVICE LEVELS AND SUPPORT
8.1 Provider will make the Service available 99.9% of the time each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance announced at least 48 hours in advance.
8.2 If monthly availability falls below 99.9%, Customer is entitled to escalating service credits: 5% of the monthly fee for availability between 99.0% and 99.9%; 10% for availability between 98.0% and 99.0%; and 25% for availability below 98.0%.
8.3 Service credits are Customer's exclusive remedy for Provider's failure to meet the availability commitment in Section 8.1; they do not limit remedies for any other claim.

9. WARRANTIES
9.1 Each party warrants that it has authority to enter into this Agreement, and Provider warrants that the Service will perform materially as described in its documentation. All other warranties are disclaimed to the extent permitted by law.

10. INDEMNIFICATION
10.1 Provider shall defend and indemnify Customer against any third-party claim alleging that the Service infringes a patent, copyright, or trademark, or misappropriates a trade secret, and shall pay all damages, losses, and reasonable attorneys' fees finally awarded or agreed in settlement with respect to such claim. This indemnity is not subject to the limitations in Section 11.
10.2 Customer shall defend and indemnify Provider against any third-party claim arising from Customer Content or Customer's use of the Service in violation of law or this Agreement.
10.3 The indemnifying party shall have sole control of the defense and settlement of any indemnified claim, and the indemnified party shall provide reasonable cooperation at the indemnifying party's expense.

11. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
11.1 EXCEPT AS SET FORTH IN SECTIONS 11.2 AND 11.3, EACH PARTY'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.
11.2 The limitation in Section 11.1 does not apply to a party's breach of its confidentiality obligations under Section 13 or to the parties' indemnification obligations under Section 10.
11.3 For claims arising out of a security incident, each party's total aggregate liability shall not exceed two (2) times the fees paid or payable by Customer in the twelve (12) months preceding the incident.
11.4 NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES, OR FOR LOST PROFITS OR REVENUE, EXCEPT WITH RESPECT TO THE OBLIGATIONS DESCRIBED IN SECTIONS 11.2 AND 11.3.

12. TERMINATION
12.1 Either party may terminate this Agreement for the other party's material breach that remains uncured thirty (30) days after written notice describing the breach.
12.2 For thirty (30) days following expiration or termination, Provider will make Customer Content available to Customer for export in a commonly used, machine-readable format, after which Provider will delete Customer Content in accordance with the DPA.

13. CONFIDENTIALITY
13.1 Each party will protect the other party's confidential information using at least reasonable care, use it only to perform under this Agreement, and disclose it only to personnel and advisors with a need to know, subject to customary exclusions and compelled-disclosure exceptions.

14.1 This Agreement, the Order Form, and the DPA constitute the entire agreement between the parties. Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other's consent, except to a successor in a merger or sale of substantially all assets. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of New York. Notices must be in writing.
